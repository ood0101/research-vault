### B) APPROACH ANALYSIS -- WHAT WAS TRIED BEFORE

#### The Problem Space: Modeling Biology with AI

**What existed before GenBio AI:**

1. **Single-modality models (the siloed approach):**
   - AlphaFold / AlphaFold2 (DeepMind/Isomorphic Labs) -- protein structure prediction from sequence. Revolutionary but limited to structure prediction; does not model DNA, RNA, cells, or multi-scale interactions
   - ESM / ESM2 (Meta AI) -- protein language models, up to 15B parameters. Good at protein tasks but not connected to other biological modalities
   - Geneformer (2023) -- single-cell foundation model for gene networks
   - scGPT -- single-cell generative pre-trained transformer
   - scFoundation -- single-cell foundation model

2. **Why they fell short:**
   - Biology is multi-scale: DNA encodes RNA, RNA makes proteins, proteins form cells, cells form tissues. Modeling each layer in isolation misses cross-scale interactions
   - "Research silo problem" -- each model was built by a different team, using different architectures, different data formats, different evaluation standards
   - Most models handled 1-2 tasks, not hundreds
   - Many single-cell models truncate or sample the transcriptome rather than processing it whole

3. **The "integration gap":**
   - Microsoft Research study showed single-cell foundation models "do not actually develop a deep understanding of biological tasks"
   - Simple statistical methods continue to outperform deep learning in predicting transcriptomic profiles in many cases
   - Paper "No Foundations without Foundations" argues purely data-driven architectures are insufficient; semi-mechanistic frameworks are needed

**GenBio AI's distinctive approach:**
- **Modular but integrated:** Build separate foundation models for DNA, RNA, protein, protein structure, single-cell, evolutionary information -- but design them from the start to interact with each other
- **Scale + efficiency:** MoE (Mixture of Experts) architecture for protein (16B params) achieves better performance than ESM2-15B with better efficiency
- **Multi-task:** 300+ tasks simultaneously vs. 1-2 for competitors
- **Full transcriptome processing:** AIDO.Cell processes entire human transcriptome without truncation/sampling -- a first

#### Why Now -- The Convergence

1. **Data explosion:** Tens of millions of single-cell datasets now in public domain. Human Protein Atlas, HuBMAP, and other consortia have generated unprecedented biological data at all scales
2. **Transformer architectures:** Proven in NLP (GPT) and vision, now adaptable to biological sequences (DNA, RNA, protein can be treated as "languages")
3. **Compute availability:** Cloud GPU/TPU infrastructure (GenBio uses Google Cloud) makes training 7B-16B parameter models feasible for startups
4. **Regulatory signals:** FDA announcing plans to phase out animal testing creates demand for in silico alternatives
5. **AlphaFold's proof of concept:** Showed AI could solve a 50-year biology problem (protein folding), opening the door for broader biological AI ambitions
6. **Team convergence:** The specific people (Baker, Xing, Song, Bar-Joseph, Lundberg, Segal, Theis) have individually built key pieces of this puzzle over decades; GenBio AI brings them together

### C) THIRD-PARTY & SKEPTIC COMMENTARY

#### Direct GenBio AI Criticism
- **No targeted public criticism found.** GenBio AI is relatively new (founded 2024) and has not yet attracted focused skeptical commentary. This is itself a data point -- the company has not been around long enough for critics to evaluate results.

#### Broader Foundation Models in Biology Skepticism

1. **Microsoft Research (2024):** Zero-shot evaluation reveals that single-cell foundation models "have a long way to go" and don't develop deep understanding of biological tasks. scGPT has "limited ability to predict held-out gene expression."

2. **"No Foundations without Foundations" (arXiv, Jan 2025):** Argues "the dream of foundation models in regulatory biology will remain elusive unless grounded in a biologically informed, semi-mechanistic framework." Simple methods still outperform deep learning for many transcriptomic prediction tasks.

3. **Nature Scientific Reports (2025):** "Generative AI lacks the human creativity to achieve scientific discovery from scratch... unable to generate truly original hypotheses and incapable of having an epiphany to detect anomalies in experimental results."

4. **GenBio AI's own benchmark paper acknowledges:** "FM-based models often struggle to outperform simpler baselines" in perturbation response modeling (ICML 2025 paper by Hasanaj et al.)

5. **Gary Marcus:** General AI skeptic who argues LLMs are "inherently broken" and won't deliver on grand promises. While not specific to GenBio, his critiques apply to the broader approach.

6. **ETC Group (ethical concerns):** Raises biopiracy and transparency concerns about generative biology broadly -- products derived from GenBio may be poorly labeled, algorithmic errors could produce unexpected side effects.

7. **Mohammed AlQuraishi (Columbia University):** Estimates "the first virtual cell could arrive in 15 years or less" -- suggesting even optimists think this is a long-term challenge.

#### Genuine Technical Risks (Even Supporters Acknowledge)

- **Generalization vs. specialization trade-off:** Foundation models generalize but may lose specificity needed for real drug discovery
- **Hallucination risk:** In medical/biological contexts, plausible-but-wrong outputs are dangerous
- **Data quality and bias:** Models inherit biases from training data; certain populations may be underrepresented
- **Benchmarking immaturity:** Evaluation standards for biological foundation models are still emerging; hard to know if reported metrics translate to real-world value
- **Integration challenge:** Building individual models is hard; making them work together coherently is an order of magnitude harder. Recursion's CEO: "I think that's likely to be the biggest challenge that we face: building the single model."
- **Commercial validation gap:** No publicly announced pharma partnerships, customers, or revenue as of March 2026
